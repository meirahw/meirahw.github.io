Welcome! I am a doctoral student in the Department of Geography & Environmental Studies at the University of New Mexico working with Dr. Yolanda C. Lin. I received a BS in Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences from the University of Wisconsin and an MS in Geography from the University of Florida. After graduating with my MS, I worked at the Illinois State Water Survey for three years, where I collaborated with communities in rural Illinois to help them become more resilient to natural and human-made hazards. My academic research interests lie at the intersection of natural hazards, risk communication, and feminist cartographies. In my free time, I enjoy hiking, cycling, playing piano, going to art/farmer's markets, and watching (or avoiding) the Chicago White Sox.
